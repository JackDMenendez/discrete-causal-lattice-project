## Decisions & flags
**Superseded notices are the author's to add.** This is the one procedure step
still outstanding and the reason a reader on an old DOI currently gets no
warning. It is flagged not as a task for the PM but so the PM does not
double-apply it or report the procedure complete. What would close it: the
author confirming the metadata edits are live on all three prior records.

**Version-vs-concept DOI.** The 07-18 procedure flag said concept; the
author's 07-20 verbatim text said version; the author confirmed **version**
when asked. So Paper I's printed citation deliberately deviates from that
flag. The distinction is real and should be preserved downstream: the
*printed* citation pins provenance (version), while *linking* metadata should
track latest (concept). If a future reader reports Paper I citing a stale
Paper IV, that is the expected trade-off of the author's choice, not a bug.

**The claim map is the live risk.** Paper I's own text is now correct, but
anything on the website derived from v1.0–v1.02 still asserts a viability that
has been withdrawn. The matter sector is the easiest thing to get wrong —
earlier framing said "dim-6 suppressed, therefore safe", and that reading is
**retracted**, not softened. Board issues #17 (gauge-sector birefringence) and
#19 (multi-channel concordance P9) are the two whose status this settles
negatively *as constructed*; their derivations stand.

**Audit-table scope.** Only one row was added and no existing row's status
changed, per the author's choice among three offered options. A fuller
reconciliation — annotating the matter-sector `PASS` rows (Dirac equation,
Spin-½) with a pointer to the revision notice — was raised by the auditor and
deliberately **not** done. If a referee later objects that a `PASS` badge sits
on a construction declared non-viable, that is the known open edge.
